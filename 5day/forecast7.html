May 7, 2020 Update:

  • The user can now do 5 or 7 day forecasts.
  • Added an optional UVI control for each day
  • Overhauled control panels
    • Added Hue, Saturation, and Lighting controls for the background color
    • Radio toggles for choosing between 5/7-day forecasts and activation of UVI panels
    • Precipitation Chance entries are now prominent
  • Sky conditions and forecast-descriptors are now handled directly on the forecast canvas
    • Hovering/clicking on the visible sky condition will cycle through the different options
    • The empty area below the sky condition is where the cycle-control is for the descriptor. A select few (6 or so; including no descriptor) support the display of the rain chances (percent)

Here's a little forecast generator for you. I've included all sorts of controls. This includes the City Name, Days, Background, Sky Conditions, Descriptors, and Temperature modification. Currently, I don't know of a way to quick/easy way to save a screen-shot of the forecast. But you can simply use your "print screen" button to copy a total screen-shot to the virtual clipboard, then paste inside of a paint program to crop. Windows 10 users can simply press ⊞ Win + ⇧ Shift + S to draw a screenshot box around the forecast. Other options include a snipping tool or Greenshot.

This took me a little while to make it work, but a lot of the time I was learning SVG's, creating the artwork, and fixing simple mental errors. Feel free to use this tool to make custom forecasts that you can share, silly or not. It's a great tool that parents and/or teachers can use to assist kids with making a forecast. Feel free to use/modify in any way you like. All I ask is that you include credit in your code.
