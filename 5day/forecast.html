27 JAN 2021: Major update (mostly)

  • Implementation of HTML5 Canvas graphics. This allows the direct download/save of the canvas, without the need to do any screenshots. You can adjust the output size of the image as well. As always, you can still screenshot it if you'd like.
  • Dynamic-Sizing and Mobile-Friendly. The canvas will automatically resize to fit the screen of your device. It has also been optimized so it should work for tablets and phones. If you are using a phone, you must use landscape mode for it to work right.
  • New random temperature generation algorithm. Some functions were included to generate time-of-year based random temperatures (roughly using climatological averages) for a mostly-temperate mid-latitude climate. The new Hemisphere feature takes advantage of this, to reflect approximated time-of-year averages.
  • New Title Display Modes. Tweak how you like the look of the title of your forecast. Include/exclude your name or city, or use your own description in custom mode.
  • New Wind and UVI toggles

Previous Updates:

  • 07 MAY 2020: Choose between 5 or 7 day forecasts; interactive canvas

Use this forecast generator to make/publish/share your own predictions. If you like forecasting, it's a nice simple way of making a graphical representation for your audience/peers. Parents and/or teachers can use it to assist their kids to make their own forecasts, and who knows, maybe spark an interest in science or meteorology.

Thank you for your feedback as well. In general, much of the functionality is influenced by user-feedback/requests which has allowed me an opportunity to expand my programming understanding. It is humbling to know that something I've made and worked at for a long time has benefited others.

Thanks to GoalKicker.com for some very useful guides on programming (HTML5, Canvas, JS, CSS guides assisted here).
