* Updated thru 27 NOV 2020.
-- Upon issuance of the official HURDAT2 update (generally in proximity to the start of Hurricane Season), this site will be cleared to prepare for the upcoming season.

* Information presented here is retrieved from current-season 'best-track' (https://ftp.nhc.noaa.gov/atcf/btk/) data. Because landfall-data are not recorded in the best-track data, they are compiled using the advisory archive (https://ftp.nhc.noaa.gov/atcf/adv). GIS software has also been used at times to estimate landfall locale and timing.

In the future, potential landfalls over small islands will not be considered due to the highly-variable nature with any change in the preliminary data and general lack of accuracy stemming from coordinate data being rounded to the nearest 10th of a degree.

Wind-extent information is currently not included. As such, 'null' space-fillers (-999) are used where applicable.

Precision is not the aim of landfall inclusion (especially the interpolated ones) but were pursued to provide homogeneity and temporary inclusion with the official HURDAT2 record. The official record is updated during the Spring of each year, prior to the start of Hurricane Season.

Feel free to use this output in any way you'd like. My only request of it is that if used publicly, you must provide the disclaimer that the data, though mostly retrieved from NOAA, is not NOAA-official, provided, nor generated/u>. My primary purpose for this is for use in my hurdat2parser program by concatenating it with the official data. One can then compare the current-season to previous years using a supplied function that ranks partial seasons.
